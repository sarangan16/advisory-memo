[ Background ]

Grünwald Kapital AG concluded fiscal year 2024 with total assets under advisory of €4.2 billion, representing a contraction of 8.3% relative to the prior period. This reduction reflects a structural reallocation within the firm's institutional client base rather than net redemption, driven primarily by the European Central Bank's accelerated rate normalisation between Q2 and Q4 2024.

The firm's current allocation model — weighted toward long-duration sovereign debt and infrastructure debt instruments — was calibrated for the low-rate environment that persisted from 2015 through 2021. As terminal rate expectations have stabilised near 3.25%, this model requires material revision to preserve the risk-adjusted return profile communicated to institutional mandates.

[ Analysis ]

Three structural pressures bear directly on the firm's near-term positioning. First, duration exposure across the core fixed-income book remains 1.4 years in excess of the peer median, amplifying mark-to-market sensitivity to any further upward revision in rate expectations. Second, the firm's real assets sleeve — representing 18% of total AuA — carries a disproportionate concentration in logistics infrastructure contracted before the post-pandemic repricing, reducing realisation optionality through 2027. Third, fee compression in the mid-market advisory segment has reduced blended management fee yield by 14 basis points since 2022.

The window for orderly rebalancing remains open — but it is narrowing as peer institutions complete their own repositioning ahead of Q3 mandate reviews.

Competitor analysis across seven comparable advisory firms indicates that those who accelerated duration reduction in H1 2024 have, on average, recovered 62% of prior-period outperformance relative to benchmark within two reporting periods. The case for action is empirically supported.

[ Recommendation ]

The Advisory Committee recommends the following actions for Board ratification, sequenced by implementation priority:

  • 01 Initiate duration reduction in the sovereign debt sleeve, targeting a weighted average duration of 5.2 years by end of Q3 2025, through a combination of partial disposal and roll-off management. This action requires no client mandate amendment.
  • 02 Engage the three largest institutional clients — representing 44% of AuA — in bilateral strategic reviews prior to the September mandate renewal window, with a view to formalising revised return targets and risk parameters reflective of the current rate environment.
  • 03 Commission an independent valuation of the logistics real assets position to establish a credible exit range, with a recommendation to the Investment Committee by 31 July 2025 on partial disposition or secondary market transfer.
  • 04 Appoint a working group to review the mid-market advisory fee structure against current market rates, with a mandate to present revised pricing proposals to the Board no later than the October 2025 session.
