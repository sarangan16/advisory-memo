[ Background ]

"I have served this board for eleven years. The organisation now requires a chair whose horizon extends beyond my own."
— Prof. Werner Kastner, letter of resignation, 14 February 2025

Prof. Werner Kastner notified the Nomination Committee of his intention to step down as Chair of the Supervisory Board effective 30 June 2025, citing age and a desire to allow a planned transition rather than an abrupt one. His tenure has been defined by the firm's successful dual listing in 2019 and its navigation of the 2022 supply chain restructuring.

The committee has requested that Veltmann & Partner advise on the suitability of three identified candidates and make a clear recommendation prior to the May shareholder assembly, at which a formal appointment must be ratified.

[ Candidate Assessment ]
Dr. Ingrid Falkenberg
Former CFO, Rhenania Industriewerke GmbH
Age57 Board experience14 years IndependenceConfirmed

Dr. Falkenberg brings the deepest financial governance experience of the three candidates, having chaired audit committees at two DAX-adjacent firms. Her approach to board leadership is consensual without being indecisive — a distinction the committee should weigh carefully given the structural decisions anticipated over the next term.

Her sole limitation is a relative unfamiliarity with the firm's primary export markets. This is addressable through committee composition and does not constitute a material disqualifier.

[ Recommended ]
Mr. Tobias Wengler
Managing Partner, Wengler Capital Advisors
Age61 Board experience9 years IndependenceConditional

Mr. Wengler presents a credible profile but carries a conditional independence designation due to his firm's prior advisory relationship with Hartmann & Bleich AG between 2018 and 2021. While this relationship has concluded, its recency falls within the standard look-back period applied by institutional shareholders.

His candidacy would likely draw scrutiny at the shareholder assembly and risks undermining the committee's broader governance narrative at a sensitive moment in the firm's public profile.

[ Reserve ]
Ms. Claudia Reimer-Brandt
Independent Non-Executive Director
Age53 Board experience6 years IndependenceConfirmed

Ms. Reimer-Brandt is the strongest candidate on the dimension of strategic transformation, having guided two mid-cap firms through significant operational restructuring. However, her supervisory board experience, while growing, does not yet match the depth required for a chair role at a firm of this complexity and public profile.

The committee may wish to retain her as a board member with a view to chair candidacy in a subsequent term.

[ Reserve ]

[ Recommendation ]

The Advisory Committee recommends the appointment of Dr. Ingrid Falkenberg as incoming Chair of the Supervisory Board, effective 1 July 2025, subject to ratification at the May shareholder assembly.

Her governance record, independence status, and temperament represent the most defensible appointment available to the committee. The firm is entering a period that will require steady, credible oversight rather than transformational leadership — Dr. Falkenberg is suited precisely to this moment.

It is further recommended that Ms. Reimer-Brandt be formally invited to join the board as a non-executive member, establishing continuity of institutional knowledge for a future succession cycle.
